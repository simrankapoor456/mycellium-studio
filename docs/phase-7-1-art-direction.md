# Phase 7.1 art direction

## Core idea

Mycellium Studio is a living product-intelligence environment. A concentrated spark becomes a connected product system: signal, context, decisions, architecture, and an editable Product Blueprint. The interface should feel grown and precise, never rustic or decorative.

Public pages are cinematic and environmental. Product pages are calm, spatial, and information-led. Both use the same light, type, mark, and surface logic.

## Visual hierarchy

- One dominant idea per public viewport, with asymmetry and deliberate negative space.
- A floating capsule holds public navigation without becoming a glass-heavy toolbar.
- The landing story moves from spark to connected context, foundation, architecture, and portable output.
- Product screens use a clear sequence: location, project state, primary work, contextual actions.
- Lime identifies primary action, active intelligence, selection, and meaningful completion only.

## Type

- Brand display: a legally usable editorial serif for selected public headlines and the wordmark.
- Product UI and body: a clear sans-serif with stable metrics and high legibility.
- System metadata: a restrained mono face for engine mode, lineage, state, and artifact labels only.
- Public type can use fluid scale. Authenticated UI uses a compact fixed scale.
- Body measure stays near 65 to 72 characters, with increased line height on dark surfaces.

## Palette and light

- Deep environment: near-black forest and black-green depth.
- Base workspace: deep pine.
- Content surfaces: quiet elevated forest with opaque fallbacks.
- Primary text: warm off-white.
- Secondary text: muted sage.
- Intelligence accent: bioluminescent lime.
- Deliberate emphasis: restrained antique gold.
- Warning: amber for real blockers only.
- Destructive and error: red reserved for genuine failure or removal.

Lighting originates from the product graph or mark. It appears as restrained radial illumination, thin active paths, and local highlights. Large blur layers, neon outlines, and decorative glow are excluded.

## Surface hierarchy

1. Environmental background
2. Base workspace
3. Content surface
4. Elevated inspector or menu
5. Modal or critical overlay
6. Intelligence-active surface

Only navigation, menus, and exceptional intelligence moments use translucency. Product content remains readable on quiet opaque surfaces.

## Background and imagery

- The landing hero uses a code-based organic environment built from gradients, masks, grain, and the real animated product mark.
- The product visualization is an actual accessible component, not a rendered mock interface.
- Product workspaces use faint paths and local light without detailed imagery behind text.
- Grain is fixed, subtle, pointer-ignored, and disabled where it harms performance or contrast.

## Logo and icons

- Production mark: compact radial root-network with a central intelligence node, limited branching, strong silhouette, and a simplified tiny-size form.
- Wordmark: editorial `Mycellium` with widely tracked `STUDIO`.
- SVG remains the interface source. PNG exports exist only for platform icons and social media.
- The mark works without glow, in monochrome, and on light or dark surfaces.
- Product icons use one consistent line family. Custom SVG is reserved for the identity and meaningful product diagrams.

## Shape, spacing, and layout

- Buttons are pill-shaped; fields and content surfaces use a controlled medium radius.
- Public section spacing is fluid and varied. Product spacing uses a compact repeatable scale.
- Cards appear only when containment or elevation communicates hierarchy.
- Mobile layouts are recomposed into a deliberate single-column story with full-size touch targets.
- Safe areas and `100dvh` protect mobile navigation and hero stability.

## Motion

- Feedback: 120 to 180 ms with strong ease-out curves.
- Menus and overlays: 180 to 240 ms, origin-aware, and interruptible.
- Environmental transitions: 480 to 760 ms where they explain hierarchy.
- Mark sequence: Spark, Connect, Grow, Form, Settle in no more than 2.5 seconds.
- Motion uses transform and opacity, does not delay content, and never hijacks scroll.
- Reduced motion preserves all meaning with a static mark, immediate state changes, and short opacity feedback.

## Accessibility

- Body text targets WCAG AA contrast, with stronger contrast for small labels and placeholders.
- Every action has a visible focus state and at least a 44-pixel target.
- Menus, dialogs, disclosures, journey controls, and forms keep native or complete keyboard behavior.
- Status always includes text or structure, not color alone.
- Decorative atmosphere is hidden from assistive technology. Logo links receive concise accessible names.
- The system remains usable at 200 percent zoom and with reduced motion.

## Reference principles

From the supplied direction: environmental depth, a refined floating navigation capsule, centered visual gravity, restrained luminous action, editorial type contrast, and an organic network mark. The implementation does not copy the reference layout, imagery, card composition, iconography, or text.

## Material change from Phase 6

- Replace the alternating light and dark landing sections with one continuous dark environment.
- Replace the boxed split hero with an immersive mark-led composition.
- Replace repeated equal sections and tab-like workflow panels with an asymmetric lifecycle narrative.
- Replace the sticky public taskbar with a responsive floating navigation system.
- Replace the authenticated top taskbar with a calmer anchored product shell and data-backed journey container.
- Rebuild shared buttons, fields, badges, surfaces, menus, dialogs, loading states, and empty states on centralized semantic tokens.
- Replace the rooted-M identity with the selected radial network mark and production asset family.

## Explicit anti-patterns

No generic three-card feature row, fake customer proof, fake metrics, decorative percentages, gradient text, page-level theme switching, nested cards, default glass panels, heavy WebGL, autoplay media, scroll hijacking, custom cursor, permanent distracting motion, or lime used as ornament.
