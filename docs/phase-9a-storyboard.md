# Phase 9A Storyboard Interpretation

## Reference policy

Five uploaded images informed the visual direction during implementation. They were treated as private compositional references only. They are not included in the repository, production bundle, screenshots, social assets, or public page.

The implementation does not reproduce their framing, subjects, lighting, or surface detail pixel for pixel. It does not use a slideshow, image sequence, crossfade, traced photograph, watermark, or derivative raster asset. The production scene is original SVG and DOM geometry built from Mycellium's existing tokens.

## How each reference informed the result

| Reference | Observation used | Original implementation decision |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Image 1, hand introducing seeds | Inputs enter a system before they are organized. | Small product fragments begin separated above the soil. No hand or photographic planting scene is rendered. |
| Image 2, seed in dark soil under light | A seed can feel stable, quiet, and intentionally held. | The seed settles on a restrained soil horizon with limited radial light and deep forest-black depth. |
| Image 3, cracking seed | Germination should be structural, not a fade. | A crack path draws through two independent shell halves, which rotate and translate apart without bounce. |
| Image 5, early roots and small sprout | The first visible growth should remain balanced and immature. | The primary root precedes every branch; a small two-leaf shoot appears only in the final state. |
| Image 8, detailed root hierarchy | Root density is most credible when primary, secondary, and connecting paths are ordered. | One primary root produces seven child branches, followed by five later connection paths. |

## Visual translation

The photographic cues were translated into the Mycellium design language:

- dark soil became two deterministic SVG strata and sparse fixed grain
- overhead light became one bounded radial glow rather than bloom or particle effects
- seed material became two warm, precise vector shell paths
- fine roots became pale vector strokes with non-scaling line weights
- biological hierarchy became product lineage
- the final root network became the seven-area Foundation model
- lime remains reserved for meaningful activation and confirmed structure

## Continuity rules

Every beat inherits geometry from the beat before it:

1. Fragments occupy the same scene that later holds the seed.
2. Useful fragments move toward the seed position.
3. The seed opens in place.
4. The radicle begins at the seed crack.
5. The primary root starts at the radicle.
6. Every evidence branch begins on the primary root.
7. Every connection begins after both related branches exist.
8. Foundation nodes sit at existing root endpoints.
9. The final composition strengthens existing geometry instead of replacing it.

These rules prevent the sequence from reading as independent slides.

## Product meaning

The visual is not a decorative plant lifecycle. Its transformation is:

fragmented context -> selected intent -> evidence lineage -> connected decisions -> reviewable Foundation

The final state remains mixed. Some areas are confirmed, some emerging, one unknown, and one blocked. This preserves Mycellium's product promise that uncertainty remains visible and human review retains authority.

## Phase 9B boundary

The storyboard ends at a stable root network and small shoot. A later Phase 9B may begin from the final Foundation nodes and translate them into architecture responsibilities. It must not retroactively add architecture, a Blueprint, mature growth, fruit, or renewal to this Phase 9A sequence.
